A Guide to Documenting Your Art Exhibition from Start to Finish

Professional Documentation Strategy From Planning Through Post-Event Archival Essential for Gallery Success

By THE LIGHTROOM STUDIO

You've spent months planning your exhibition. The artwork is curated, the gallery is booked, the invitations are sent. Then the show opens, runs for six weeks, and closes—and you realize you have three mediocre iPhone photos to prove it ever existed.

This happens to more artists and galleries than you'd think. After documenting over 150 exhibitions, the data shows that comprehensive documentation isn't something you add at the last minute. It's a strategic process that starts weeks before opening and continues through the entire exhibition lifecycle.

Professional documentation serves multiple critical purposes: archival record-keeping for future exhibitions and grant applications, marketing assets for promoting future shows, and sales materials showing artwork in professional context to potential buyers. The exhibitions you document thoroughly become permanent parts of your professional record—the ones you don't disappear from history.
